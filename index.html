Quick Help

Beginner Level

The key for you to start scoring at the beginner level of volleyball is a good serve (I would recommend a low, overhand float serve). As a referee, I have seen many beginner level games decided by one or two long runs (a streak of points). This is usually because a player is consistently serving at a level that the other team cannot pass.

This is the perfect time for you to start building passing fundamentals, including footwork, communication (calling seems and for the ball), and proper technique. This will put you a step ahead of someone who only has a good serve and prevent you from forming bad habits later on.

I would strongly recommend learning the rules of volleyball and the basics of rotation to once again prevent bad habits and increase your overall knowledge of the sport.

Intermediate Level

At this point, you should know the basics of passing and properly understand the game of volleyball. The next step is to determine your position in the system. There are 6 positions:
OH - Outside Hitter
OPP - Opposite (Right-side) Hitter
MB - Middle Blocker
S - Setter
L - Libero
DS/SS - Defensive/Serving Specialist

If you haven't already, I would highly recommend working out (specifically lower body and plyometrics for defense and jumping). Having a stronger body results in more control over yourself and over the volleyball.

If possible, learn how to dive. Having this ability allows a defender to get as close to the ground as possible, leading to more digs and saves.

Understand blocking. Always go up with a reason, whether it be to take away court space from the hitter or to attack the attacker. A good blocker can adapt to an attacker and effectively communicate with their defense.

The intermediate level ramps up the level of communication throughout the game. The more knowledge you can share with your team, the better prepared your team will be (some examples are calling out whether the setter is front or back row, and where the hitters are lining up).

Advanced Level

At the advanced level, there is no limit to how far you can improve your ability. Physically, you can always be more explosive and controlled. Mentally, you can always be more calculating and concetrated. In volleyball, there are so many different ways to hone your style that it is impossible to do it all. Therefore, you must pick and choose. Build your own toolkit and play for fun.

As an outside hitter, my love for the game is hitting the ball as hard as I can and getting aces on the serving line. So I took the time to develop an advanced level top spin jump serve and practiced using my whole body when spiking the ball. Once you have defined your skill set, there are a few general parts of the game that are helpful as you face better competition.

Learn how to read the hitter. This will allow you to move faster on defense and get more digs. Learn advanced blocking. A common form of blocking in beach volleyball volleyball is dive blocking. This form of blocking can help in single blocker scenarios turning the block into an attack. Triple blocks are also common at the highest level. Learn how to play the game faster. Being able to see the ball earlier or starting your forward motion before the ball is struck can dramatically increase your movement speed and range of getable balls.
